1983 - The Townsville Community Music Centre was established at a public meeting on May 24th. Presented a cheque for $50. It was the Centre's first funds.
1984 – Mira Henderson was the first Acting Director. She instigated a series of classes using the skills of people living in Townsville to enhance and encourage music learners of all ages.







1985 – Produced the first musical of Benjamin Britten Noyes Flude in St. James Anglican Cathedral
1987 – First fund raising dinner. It became a tradition as it was a wonderful mixture of good food, music and wine.
1990 – First lunchtime concert was held at PercTucker Gallery. It paves the way for
performance opportunities for emerging musicians.
2003 – A spectacular variety concert was held at Townsville Civic Theatre to celebrate
twenty years of the Music Centre and a tribute to Mary Lou, former administrator of
the center who retired due to ill health.

2005 – The cenre moved office in the Old Magistrate Court.
2008 – Had it first coffee table book by Jean Dartnall titled “Townsville Community Music Centre: some memories of the first 25 years
2012 – The centre moved to its present office in the Townsville Civic Theatre building
